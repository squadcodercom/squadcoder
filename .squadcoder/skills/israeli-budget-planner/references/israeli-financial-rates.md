## Mortgage (Mashkanta) Guidelines
### BOI Regulations
- Max LTV: 75% (first home), 70% (home replacement), 50% (investment)
- Mix rule: a minimum of 1/3 of the total mortgage must be fixed-unlinked
- Up to 2/3 of the total mortgage may be variable/prime
- The old 1/3-prime cap was removed in January 2021

### Typical Rate Ranges (2026)
| Track | Type | Range |
|-------|------|-------|
| Prime-linked | Variable | Prime +/- 0.5% |
| Fixed unlinked | Fixed | 4.5%-6.5% |
| CPI-linked fixed | Fixed + CPI | 3.0%-5.0% + CPI |
| CPI-linked variable | Resets 5yr | 2.5%-4.5% + CPI |

### Payment-to-Income (PTI)
- BOI regulatory cap: monthly repayment may not exceed 50% of disposable income
- Banks usually decline applications above 40% of disposable income

## Net-Pay Deductions (Employee, 2026)
These feed the affordability math (net income drives PTI and budget headroom). Rates are the salaried-employee shares, verified against Bituach Leumi (btl.gov.il).
- Bituach Leumi (employee): 1.04% on the part of salary up to the reduced-collection step, 7% above it. The reduced rate rose to 1.04% under Amendment 252 (effective 2026).
- Health-tax (dmei bituach briut, employee): 3.23% up to the reduced-collection step, 5.17% above it.
- Reduced-collection step (60% of average wage): 7,703 NIS/month (from 1 Jan 2026); both BL and health-tax bands split here.
- Maximum income subject to insurance (ceiling): 51,910 NIS/month (from 1 Jan 2026).
- Income tax: marginal 10%-50% brackets, widened retroactively from 1 Jan 2026 (the 20% step runs to 228,000 NIS/year and the 31% step to 301,200 NIS/year).
- Tax credit point (nekudat zikui): 242 NIS/month (2026); resident base 2.25, women +0.5.
- Pension contribution (employee): 6% minimum, 6.5% also common.

## Arnona (Municipal Property Tax)
Arnona is set by each municipality and updated annually via the tzav arnona (arnona order). Per-city tables go stale within a year or two, so do not hardcode them here. To get an accurate figure:
- Fetch the relevant municipality's current-year tzav arnona (2026) from its website.
- Note the rate is per square meter, varies by zone and property classification, and is billed bi-monthly in most cities.
- For housing indices and inflation context, use the israeli-cbs MCP (cbs.gov.il).
