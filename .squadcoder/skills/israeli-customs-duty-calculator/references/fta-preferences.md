# Israeli FTA Preferences Cheat Sheet

Israel has signed free-trade agreements with most of its major trading partners. A valid origin proof removes the customs duty (not VAT or purchase tax).

## US-Israel Free Trade Agreement (1985)

- First US bilateral FTA.
- Origin rule: sum of US materials cost and direct US processing costs must be at least 35 percent of the appraised value.
- Origin proof: US Origin Invoice Declaration stated on the commercial invoice (or shipping list / proforma). Green Form (Form A) discontinued January 10, 2018.
- Signature: the US exporter or manufacturer must sign. Verification may be requested by Israeli Customs.

Source: https://www.trade.gov/us-israel-free-trade-agreement

## EU-Israel Association Agreement

- Entered into force June 2000.
- Rules of origin: Pan-Euro-Mediterranean (PEM) Convention, 2012 version. Israel has NOT yet ratified the revised PEM Convention and continues to use the 2012 rules.
- Origin proof:
  - EUR.1 movement certificate, stamped by the exporter's customs authority. Wet-ink signature required; Israel does not accept electronic signatures.
  - Invoice declaration accepted for shipments up to 6000 euros by any exporter, or any value by an approved exporter.
- EUR-MED certificates: not needed (Israel is not part of the revised regime).

Source: https://trade.ec.europa.eu/access-to-markets/en/content/rules-origin-revised-pan-euro-mediterranean-convention

## UK-Israel Trade and Partnership Agreement (2019)

- Signed post-Brexit, mirrors the EU agreement rules.
- Origin proof: EUR.1 or invoice declaration up to 6000 euros.
- Approved-exporter status is available for frequent exporters.

Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/summary-of-the-uk-israel-trade-and-partnership-agreement

## Canada-Israel FTA (CIFTA, modernized September 1, 2019)

- Original CIFTA in force since 1997; modernized version entered into force September 1, 2019.
- Origin proof: Form B239 (Canada-Israel FTA Certificate of Origin).
- Validity: four years from signature.

Source: https://www.cbsa-asfc.gc.ca/publications/dm-md/d11/d11-5-6-eng.html

## EFTA-Israel (Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein)

- Origin proof: EUR.1 movement certificate.

## Mercosur-Israel

- In force; origin proof: Mercosur-Israel movement certificate.

## Common pitfalls

- Invoice declaration on a shipment above 6000 euros without approved-exporter status: rejected.
- EUR.1 signed electronically: rejected by Israeli Customs.
- Non-originating components exceed the de minimis tolerance: product loses origin.
- Wrong origin rule applied (textile rules are different from general industrial rules).
- Supplier declaration missing for cumulation: origin cannot be proved.
