# Pension Tax Benefits in Israel

## Employee Tax Benefits

### Tax Credit (Zikui Mas)
- 35% tax credit on employee pension contributions
- Ceiling: 8,148 NIS/year (679 NIS/month, 2026); the 679 figure equals 7% of qualifying salary 9,700 NIS
- Credit directly reduces tax liability
- Example: qualifying salary 9,700 NIS/month × 7% = 679 NIS/month contribution. Tax credit: 679 × 35% = 238 NIS/month tax reduction (~2,852 NIS/year)
- Contributions above 679 NIS/month earn deduction, not credit, up to the further deduction ceiling

### Tax Deduction (Nikui Mas)
- Employer's pension contribution is not taxed as income for the employee
- Up to 7.5% of salary (combined pension + severance)
- Reduces taxable income, not tax directly
- Benefit depends on marginal tax rate

## Self-Employed Tax Benefits

### Mandatory Pension Obligation (2016 Law)

#### Contribution Rates
- 4.45% on net taxable income up to 6,884.50 NIS/month (half average wage)
- 12.55% on income from 6,884.50 to 13,769 NIS/month (full average wage)
- No obligation on income above full average wage
- Maximum annual mandatory obligation: ~14,044 NIS
- Obligation applies between age 21 and legal retirement age
- The first calendar year of עוסק activity is exempt
- Calculated on net taxable income (after deductions), not gross revenue

#### Tax Benefit Rates on Voluntary Pension Contributions
- First 5.5% of qualifying income: Tax credit at 35% (up to 12,804 NIS/year)
- Additional 11% of qualifying income: Tax deduction (up to 25,608 NIS/year)
- Maximum deductible pension contribution: 38,412 NIS/year (16.5% of income up to 232,800 NIS)
- Qualifying income ceiling for pension: 232,800 NIS/year (19,400 NIS/month, 2026)

### Keren Hishtalmut
- Tax deduction: up to 13,203 NIS/year (4.5% of income up to 293,397 NIS)
- Profit-exempt ceiling: 20,566 NIS/year
- Gains tax-free after 6 years

## Universal Benefits

### Keren Hishtalmut Gains
- After 6-year lock-in: ALL gains are tax-free
- No capital gains tax on withdrawal
- This is unique to the Israeli tax system
- For employees: both employee and employer portions, up to the salary cap
- 2024 Treasury proposal to limit this benefit has not been enacted as of May 2026; monitor for renewed legislative activity

### Pension Payout Tax Exemption (Tikun 190 schedule)
- Monthly pension partially tax-exempt under Amendment 190 to the Income Tax Ordinance (2012)
- 2026 exemption rate: 57.5% (rising to 62.5% in 2027, 67% from 2028)
- Tax-free amount: up to 5,422 NIS/month pension income (2026)
- Qualifying pension threshold (kitzbah mezakah): 9,430 NIS/month
- Tax-exempt severance ceiling: 13,750 NIS per year of service (2026)

### Tikun 190 Deposits (post-60 tax shelter)
The other half of Amendment 190: lets pensioners 60+ with a qualifying kitzbah deposit any amount into a kupat gemel le-kitzbah with dramatic tax improvement.

- **Eligibility:** Age 60+ AND already drawing kitzbah of at least the minimum threshold (~5,012 NIS/month in 2026)
- **No annual or lifetime deposit cap**
- **Withdrawal as monthly kitzbah:** Fully exempt from income tax for life
- **Withdrawal as lump sum:** 15% tax on the real (CPI-adjusted) GAIN only; principal not taxed
- **Standard use cases:** severance at retirement, sale of business proceeds, retirement bonus, inheritance, excess liquid savings
- **Comparison:** A 1M NIS deposit drawn as lump sum after 10 years (50% real gain = 500K) pays 15% × 500K = 75K NIS tax; the same money in a brokerage account would pay marginal rate (up to 47%) on the gain
- **Estate planning:** Funds pass to designated beneficiaries under fund rules
- **PFIC caveat:** US persons should consult a tax professional; kupot gemel may be PFICs under US tax law

### Heichum Kitzbah (the other use of "Tikun 190")
- The same 2012 amendment introduced the heichum kitzbah mechanism that links severance withdrawal to the future kitzbah-mezakah exemption pool
- Each shekel of tax-exempt severance withdrawn within 32 years before retirement reduces the lifetime kitzbah-mezakah pool by 1.35 shekels
- Lifetime kitzbah-mezakah pool ≈ 32 years × 180% × kitzbah mezakah (currently ~1.5M NIS for a 2026 retiree)
- Withdrawals outside the 32-year window have no impact

## Section 14 (Severance Arrangement, Severance Pay Law 1963)
- Employer ongoing contributions at 8.33% of salary satisfy full severance obligation
- Employee keeps accumulated pension even if terminated
- Employer released from additional severance payment in the fully-funded sub-case only
- Four sub-cases under the General Approval of 30 June 1998: full coverage; partial-salary coverage; delayed start (employer owes gap at final salary); collective-agreement variants
- Mandatory pension law requires only 6% for severance component; the 2.33% gap (8.33% - 6%) is the employer's exposure in the partially-funded sub-cases, calculated at final salary

## Tax Optimization Tips
1. Maximize keren hishtalmut contributions (best tax-free vehicle)
2. Ensure pension contributions reach the tax credit ceiling
3. Self-employed: maximize deductible pension contributions
4. Review management fees (even 0.5% difference compounds significantly)
5. Consider Tikun 190 deposits at age 60+ for any lump-sum windfall (severance, inheritance, sale of business)
6. Time severance withdrawal carefully: heichum kitzbah penalty is zero outside the 32-year window
