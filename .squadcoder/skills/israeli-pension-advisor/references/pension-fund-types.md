# Israeli Pension Fund Types

## Keren Pensia (Pension Fund)
- **Type:** Defined contribution (since 1995 reform)
- **Components:** Retirement savings + disability insurance + survivors insurance
- **Management fees:** Non-default funds: max 0.5% of balance + up to 6% of deposits. Selected default funds: 0.22% of balance + 1% of deposits, locked for 10 years from each member's join date.
- **Deposit ceiling:** 5,645 NIS/month (67,740 NIS/year, 2026); 20.5% of 2× average wage. Corresponds to insurable salary cap of 27,538 NIS/month.
- **Investment:** Balanced portfolio managed by fund (multiple tracks)
- **Payout:** Monthly pension at retirement (annuity)
- **Major fund operators include** Menora Mivtachim, Migdal Makefet, Harel, Clal, Phoenix, Yelin Lapidot, Altshuler Shaham, Meitav, Mor, Infinity; the 4 default funds since Nov 2024 are Meitav, Altshuler Shaham, Mor, Infinity. (List is not exhaustive; check current Capital Markets Authority directory before recommending a specific operator.)

### Advantages
- Lower management fees
- Built-in insurance coverage (disability + survivors)
- Survivors pension under takanon achid for an ACTIVE member: spouse 60% of insured salary; orphans 40% combined (divided among children under 21, extended to 24 during military / national / regular national service); dependent parent 20%; cap 100%. (Not "30% per orphan" as some agents quote.)
- Guaranteed minimum return mechanism on a portion of assets
- Simpler structure than bituach menahalim

### Disadvantages
- Less investment flexibility than bituach menahalim
- Monthly pension only (no lump sum option for post-2008 savings)

## Bituach Menahalim (Manager's Insurance)
- **Type:** Insurance-based savings product
- **Components:** Savings + separate risk insurance (disability, life)
- **Management fees:** Up to 1.05% of balance + up to 4% of contributions
- **Investment:** Multiple tracks available (stocks, bonds, etc.)
- **Payout:** Monthly pension or partial lump sum
- **Guaranteed annuity factor (mekadem hamara mubatach):** Pre-2013 policies retain it; policies sold after 1 January 2013 do not.

### Advantages
- More investment track options
- Potential for partial lump sum withdrawal
- Pre-2013 policies' guaranteed annuity factor is a meaningful retirement benefit

### Disadvantages
- Higher management fees
- Separate risk premium reduces savings
- More complex structure
- Declining for new policies since 2013. Most new employees directed to keren pensia.

## Keren Hishtalmut (Training Fund)
- **Type:** Medium-term savings
- **Lock-in:** 6 years (3 years for accredited training/education)
- **Tax:** Gains tax-free after 6 years (for employees and self-employed within ceilings)
- **Contribution ceiling for tax benefit:** Salary ceiling for tax-free employer contribution: 15,712 NIS/month (188,544 NIS/year, 2026)
- **Most popular savings product in Israel**

### For Employees
- Employee: 2.5% of salary
- Employer: 7.5% of salary
- At the tax-free salary cap of 15,712 NIS/month, max total contribution = 1,571 NIS/month
- Employer contribution on salary above 15,712 NIS is taxable income to the employee even though it still sits in the fund

### For Self-Employed
- Two ceilings: Tax deduction from income: up to 13,203 NIS/year (4.5% of income up to 293,397 NIS/year). Profit-exempt ceiling: 20,566 NIS/year.
- Tax benefit: deduction from taxable income; gains on deposits up to 20,566 NIS are tax-free after 6 years

## Kupat Gemel le-Tagmulim (Historical Provident Fund)
- General savings/investment vehicle
- Mostly closed to new deposits since the 2008 pension reform
- Existing balances continue to grow
- Withdrawal rules: marginal tax on the GAINS (not balance) before maturity

## Kupat Gemel le-Hashka'a (IRA-style, 2016 reform)
- Self-directed retirement savings
- Annual deposit ceiling: 83,641 NIS per beneficiary for 2026 (updated annually by the Capital Markets Authority)
- Self-directed investment choice (within the fund's universe)
- Withdrawal: at marginal rate if drawn before age 60; if drawn as kitzbah at 60+ with qualifying pension prerequisite, eligible for the 15% real-gain treatment

## Kupat Gemel le-Kitzbah (Tikun 190 vehicle)
- **Eligibility:** Age 60+ and already drawing a kitzbah of at least the minimum threshold (approximately 5,012 NIS/month in 2026)
- **No deposit cap** (annual or lifetime)
- **Withdrawal as kitzbah:** Fully exempt from income tax for life
- **Withdrawal as lump sum:** 15% tax on the real (CPI-adjusted) gain only
- **Use cases:** Sale of business, retirement bonus, severance at age 60+, inheritance, surplus liquid savings
- See `references/tax-benefits.md` for the full mechanics

## Default Pension Fund System
- Tender awarded November 2024 to 4 funds; tender period runs through October 31, 2028
- ID-digit auto-assignment for employers with 50+ employees activated June 1, 2025
  - Last digits 0, 1: Meitav
  - Last digits 2, 3: Altshuler Shaham
  - Last digits 4, 5, 6: Mor
  - Last digits 7, 8, 9: Infinity
- Smaller employers may select any of the 4 funds
- Fee cap: 0.22% balance + 1% deposits, locked for 10 years from each member's join date (per-member, not tender)
- Employees can switch funds at any time after assignment
