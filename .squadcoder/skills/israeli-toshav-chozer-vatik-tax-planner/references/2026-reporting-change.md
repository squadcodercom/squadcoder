# 2026 Reporting-Obligation Change - Amendment 272

This is the #1 thing this skill must get right. The change is widely misreported on social media as "vatik benefits being canceled." That is wrong. What was canceled is the REPORTING exemption. The TAX exemption itself is fully intact.

## What changed

| Item | Pre-2026 | From 1.1.2026 |
|---|---|---|
| Tax on foreign income (vatik) | Exempt for 10 years | Exempt for 10 years (unchanged) |
| Reporting foreign income to Mas Hachnasa | Exempt - no reporting | Required (still exempt from tax) |
| Reporting foreign assets | Exempt - no reporting | Required when ITA asks |
| Foreign trust disclosures | Limited | Expanded |
| Statute basis | Sections 134b + 135(b) granted the reporting exemption | Both sections canceled by Amendment 272 |

## Effective date and applicability threshold

The change applies to individuals who become Israeli tax residents (for the first time as olim, or as toshav chozer vatik) on or after **1 January 2026**.

If the individual became an Israeli resident before 1.1.2026, they keep BOTH the tax exemption AND the reporting exemption for the remainder of their 10-year window. A returnee who became Israeli resident on 1 December 2025 enjoys the legacy regime (no reporting) until 1 December 2035.

A returnee who became Israeli resident on 2 January 2026 falls under the new regime: tax-exempt but must report.

The date is by tax-residency status, not physical arrival. See `section-14-mechanics.md` on the clock.

## What must be reported post-2026

Per Shibolet & Co. and Arnon, Tadmor-Levy commentary:

1. **Annual return Form 1301** - required.
2. **Schedule D-1 (נספח ד-1)** - all foreign-source income itemized, marked as exempt under section 14 where applicable.
3. **Capital declaration (הצהרת הון)** - foreign assets when the Tax Authority requests one (this is a standard tool the ITA already uses for resident taxpayers).
4. **Trust disclosures** - if the returnee is a settlor, beneficiary, or controlling owner of a foreign trust, expanded disclosures are now required. Israeli-resident trustees also have new annual reporting on trusts that previously did not need to file in Israel.
5. **CFC reporting** if the returnee controls a foreign company. The exemption on dividends can be challenged depending on circumstances.

## Practical implications

- **No tax owed**, but tax compliance work is now non-trivial. Expect a CPA bill where previously there was no filing at all.
- **Statute of limitations** clocks now run on properly-filed returns, which gives a closing-of-the-books benefit that the old regime did not provide.
- **OECD compliance**: the change is widely understood to be a response to OECD pressure on Israel's disclosure standards (BEPS, CRS). The Knesset Research Center analysis (2025) frames it that way.
- **Year-of-acclimation election** (section 14(b)) becomes more attractive because it can shift the residency start date past 1.1.2026 trigger (only for those who arrived in late 2025 and have flexibility on when residency commenced).

## What it is NOT

- Not a cancellation of the tax exemption.
- Not retroactive to pre-2026 returnees.
- Not a change to the years-abroad threshold (still 10 years for vatik).
- Not specific to vatik - the same change applies to olim chadashim.
