# Israeli Tax Rates and Thresholds - 2026

Last verified: April 2026

## Income Tax Brackets (2026, revised April 2026 retroactive to January)

| Bracket | Monthly (NIS) | Annual (NIS) | Rate |
|---------|--------------|-------------|------|
| 1 | Up to 7,010 | Up to 84,120 | 10% |
| 2 | 7,011 - 10,060 | 84,121 - 120,720 | 14% |
| 3 | 10,061 - 19,000 | 120,721 - 228,000 | 20% |
| 4 | 19,001 - 25,100 | 228,001 - 301,200 | 31% |
| 5 | 25,101 - 46,690 | 301,201 - 560,280 | 35% |
| 6 | 46,691 - 60,130 | 560,281 - 721,560 | 47% |

Note: Brackets 3 and 4 were widened in April 2026 (retroactive to January 2026). Previous bracket 3 ended at 16,150/month (193,800/year), bracket 4 ended at 22,440/month (269,280/year). Brackets 5 and 6 top boundaries unchanged; only their start points shifted up due to bracket 4 widening.

## Surtax (Mas Yesafim)

| Type | Threshold (Annual) | Rate |
|------|-------------------|------|
| General surtax (all income types) | Above 721,560 NIS | 3% |
| Capital income surtax (since 2025) | Above 721,560 NIS | 2% |
| **Combined for capital gains** | **Above 721,560 NIS** | **5%** |

The capital income surtax applies to: capital gains, dividends, interest, rental income, CPI linkage differentials, and passive royalties.

## Bituach Leumi (National Insurance) - Employee Portion

| Component | Reduced Rate | Full Rate | Reduced Bracket | Full Bracket |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| Bituach Leumi | 0.4% | 7.0% | Up to 7,703/month | 7,703 - 51,910/month |
| Health Insurance | 3.1% | 5.0% | Up to 7,703/month | 7,703 - 51,910/month |
| **Total** | **3.5%** | **12.0%** | | |

Maximum monthly income for BL: 51,910 NIS (income above this is exempt). The reduced-rate ceiling (60 percent of the average wage) rose to 7,703 for 2026.

## Tax Credit Points (Nekudot Zikui) - 2026

| Credit | Monthly Value (NIS) |
|--------|-------------------|
| 1 credit point | ~242 NIS |
| Israeli resident | 2.25 points |
| Woman | +0.5 points |
| Children (varies by age) | 1-2.5 points per child |

Credit points reduce income tax but do NOT apply to capital gains tax under the capital gains track.
