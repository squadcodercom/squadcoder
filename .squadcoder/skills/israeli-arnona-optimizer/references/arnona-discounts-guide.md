# Arnona Discounts Guide

## Legal Framework

Arnona discounts in Israel are governed by two layers of regulation:

1. **National regulations**: The Arrangements in the State Economy Regulations (Local Authority Arnona) set minimum discount categories that all municipalities must offer.
2. **Municipal bylaws**: Each municipality may add additional discount categories or increase discount percentages beyond the national minimum (subject to Interior Ministry approval).

## Discount Categories

### 1. Oleh Chadash (New Immigrant)

**Discount**: Up to 90% on up to 100 sqm, for 12 months that the oleh may choose within the first 24 months from the date registered as an oleh (teudat oleh / population-registry date)

**Eligibility**:
- Valid teudat oleh (immigrant certificate) from the Ministry of Aliyah and Integration
- The property must be the primary residence
- Applies to the first 100 sqm only (area above 100 sqm charged at full rate)
- Available once per lifetime per property

**Required documents**:
- Teudat oleh (original + copy)
- Teudat zehut (ID card)
- Lease agreement or property deed (tabu extract)
- Arnona account details

**Important notes**:
- The 12 discounted months can be spread across the first 24 months from the aliyah/registration date (they need not be the first 12 consecutive months), which lets the oleh time the discount to higher-rate periods
- If the oleh moves to a different municipality within the eligibility window, the discount continues in the new municipality for the remaining months
- Returning residents (toshavim chozrim) with "oleh" status from the Ministry are also eligible
- Some municipalities extend benefits beyond 12 months at reduced percentages (check locally)

### 2. Active-Duty Soldier (Chayyal Sadir)

**Discount**: Up to 100% (varies by municipality, typically 100% for lone soldiers)

**Eligibility**:
- Currently serving in the IDF in mandatory service (sherut sadir)
- Living alone (not with parents)
- The property is the soldier's sole residence

**Required documents**:
- IDF service confirmation letter (ishur sherut)
- Teudat zehut
- Lease agreement
- Declaration that the soldier lives alone

**Important notes**:
- Lone soldiers (chayalim bodedim) typically receive 100% exemption
- Soldiers living with family may receive partial discounts
- Career soldiers (sadir/keva) may receive different rates
- National service participants (sherut leumi) may also qualify in some municipalities

### 3. Senior Citizen (Ezrach Vatik)

The senior discount is structured by pension/income status, NOT by age band (65-69 vs 70+):

**25% (discretionary)**:
- Receives an old-age, survivors, or work-injury pension from Bituach Leumi. No income test.

**30% (mandatory)**:
- Income-tested: household income up to the national average wage (about 13,623 NIS/month as of Jan 2026, updated annually).

**Up to 100%**:
- Senior who also receives an income supplement (hashlamat hachnasa) within the income limit.

**Eligibility (all tiers)**:
- Israeli resident at retirement age
- Property is primary residence
- Applies to first 100 sqm

**Required documents**:
- Teudat zehut (proving age)
- Income declarations (tax assessments, pension statements, Bituach Leumi statements)
- For couples: spouse's income documentation as well
- Arnona account details

**Important notes**:
- Some municipalities offer additional elderly discounts beyond the national minimum
- Income thresholds are updated annually by the government
- Holocaust survivors aged 65+ may combine elderly status with Holocaust survivor benefits (the higher discount applies)

### 4. Disabled Person (Adam Im Mugbalut)

**Medical disability 90% or more**:
- **Discount**: Up to 40% (with a Bituach Leumi medical-disability certificate of 90%+)

**Earning incapacity (degree of אי-כושר) 75% or more, with a full monthly benefit**:
- **Discount**: Up to 80%

Note: there is no 50-74% disability band in the national regulation; the bands above are the correct ones.

**Eligibility**:
- Disability determination from the National Insurance Institute (Bituach Leumi) or the Ministry of Defense (for security-related disability)
- Property is primary residence
- Applies to first 100 sqm

**Required documents**:
- Disability determination letter from Bituach Leumi (showing percentage)
- Teudat zehut
- Arnona account details

**Important notes**:
- Mental health disabilities are included if formally determined
- Temporary disability (e.g., work injury) may qualify for the duration of the determination
- IDF-disabled veterans receive benefits through the Ministry of Defense rehabilitation division, which may include separate arnona benefits

### 5. Low Income (Hachnasa Nemukha)

**Discount**: 20% to 80% depending on income level relative to thresholds

**Income thresholds (approximate, updated annually)**:

| Family Size | Maximum Monthly Income for 80% Discount | Maximum for 40% Discount |
|-------------|----------------------------------------|--------------------------|
| Individual | 2,500 NIS | 3,500 NIS |
| Couple | 3,750 NIS | 5,200 NIS |
| 3 persons | 4,375 NIS | 6,100 NIS |
| 4 persons | 4,875 NIS | 6,800 NIS |
| 5+ persons | 5,375 NIS | 7,500 NIS |

**Required documents**:
- Tax assessments (shomot mas) for the previous year
- Pay slips (tlushei maskoret) for the last 3 months
- Bituach Leumi benefit statements
- Bank statements (some municipalities)
- Self-employed: accountant certification of income

**Important notes**:
- Income includes all sources: salary, pension, rental income, social security benefits, alimony
- Excludes: one-time payments, child allowances (kitzvat yeladim), disability stipends in some cases
- Municipalities may set different threshold levels; the above are national minimums
- Renewal required annually

### 6. Student (Student)

**Discount**: Up to 50% (varies significantly by municipality)

**Eligibility**:
- Full-time student at a recognized institution of higher education
- Living alone (not with parents)
- Income below threshold (similar to low-income thresholds)
- Age limitations may apply (typically under 30, but varies)

**Required documents**:
- Student enrollment confirmation (ishur limudim)
- Teudat zehut
- Lease agreement
- Income declaration

**Important notes**:
- Part-time students may not qualify in all municipalities
- Some municipalities limit to undergraduate students only
- Married students may qualify under different criteria
- Yeshiva students may qualify in some municipalities under similar or separate frameworks

### 7. Single Parent (Horeh Yachid)

**Discount**: Up to 20%

**Eligibility**:
- Recognized single parent status
- Primary caregiver of minor children
- Income below threshold
- Property is primary residence

**Required documents**:
- Single parent recognition (from Bituach Leumi or court order)
- Children's birth certificates
- Income documentation
- Arnona account details

### 8. Large Family (Mishpacha Brukat Yeladim)

**Discount**: Up to 20%

**Eligibility**:
- Four or more dependent children under 18
- Property is primary residence
- Income below threshold (higher than standard low-income threshold)

**Required documents**:
- Children's birth certificates or teudat zehut entries
- Income documentation
- Arnona account details

### 9. Bereaved Family (Mishpacha Shkula)

**Discount**: Up to 66%

**Eligibility**:
- Family of fallen IDF soldier, terror victim, or security forces member
- Recognition from the Ministry of Defense
- Property is primary residence

**Required documents**:
- Ministry of Defense bereaved family recognition letter
- Teudat zehut
- Arnona account details

**Important notes**:
- This is a permanent benefit, not time-limited
- Both parents and spouse/children of the fallen may qualify
- Applies to the first 100 sqm

### 10. Holocaust Survivor (Nitzolei Shoah)

**Discount**: Up to 66%

**Eligibility**:
- Recognized Holocaust survivor status
- Receiving benefits from one of the recognized frameworks (Claims Conference, German government, Austrian government, or Israeli government)

**Required documents**:
- Holocaust survivor recognition documentation
- Benefit payment confirmation
- Teudat zehut

### 11. Additional Municipal Categories

Some municipalities offer additional discounts for:
- **Reservist soldiers (miluimnikim)**: Particularly after extended reserve duty
- **Volunteer organizations**: Recognized non-profits operating in the municipality
- **Empty properties**: Partial exemption for properties vacant 6+ months (typically 30-50%)
- **New residents**: Some development towns offer introductory discounts
- **Multi-property discount**: NOT available. Each property is assessed independently.

## Application Process

### General Steps

1. **Determine eligibility**: Review the categories above and identify the applicable discount
2. **Gather documents**: Collect all required documentation (originals + copies)
3. **Visit or apply online**: Go to the municipality's arnona department or apply through their website
4. **Submit application**: Fill out the discount application form (tofes bakasha le-hanacha)
5. **Wait for approval**: Typically 30-60 days for processing
6. **Check your bill**: Verify the discount appears on the next arnona bill

### Deadlines

- **Annual renewal**: Most discounts must be renewed each year (typically January-March)
- **Mid-year applications**: Discounts can be requested mid-year; they apply from the date of application (not retroactively, unless specific circumstances allow)
- **Retroactive claims**: Some municipalities allow retroactive discount claims for up to 6 months if the qualifying condition existed but was not applied

### Combining Discounts

- **Only one discount applies at a time**. If a person qualifies for multiple discounts, the highest percentage discount is applied.
- Exception: Some municipalities allow combining the empty property exemption with other discounts in specific circumstances.

### Appeals

If a discount application is denied:
1. Request the denial in writing with the reason
2. Appeal to the arnona manager within 30 days
3. If still denied, appeal to the municipal appeals committee
4. Final recourse: Administrative Court
