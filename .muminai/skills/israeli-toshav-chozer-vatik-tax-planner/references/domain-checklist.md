# Domain Checklist - Israeli Toshav Chozer Vatik Tax Planner

Version: 1
Generated: 2026-05-23
Volatility: legislative (statute + 2026 Amendment 272)

## Must (covered in this skill)

1. **Definition of toshav chozer vatik**: 10+ consecutive years as foreign tax resident before returning. Source: [kolzchut](https://www.kolzchut.org.il/he/הטבות_במס_לתושבים_חוזרים_ששהו_בחו%22ל_מעל_10_שנים).
2. **Section 14(a) 10-year exemption** on foreign-source income (active + passive + capital gains). Source: [bshcpa](https://www.bshcpa.co.il/תושב-חוזר-ותיק/), [nevo (Ordinance)](https://www.nevo.co.il/law/84255).
3. **10-year clock starts from date of Israeli tax residency** (not date of physical return). Source: [bshcpa](https://www.bshcpa.co.il/תושב-חוזר-ותיק/).
4. **Vatik vs. regular toshav chozer** distinction (10 years vs. 6 years; full exemption vs. 5-year passive + 10-year capital gains only). Source: [kolzchut regular](https://www.kolzchut.org.il/he/הטבות_במס_לתושבים_חוזרים_ששהו_בחו%22ל_בין_6_ל-10_שנים).
5. **Reporting-obligation change in 2026**: Amendment 272 cancels sections 134b and 135(b); reporting exemption gone from 1.1.2026; tax exemption remains. Applies only to those becoming residents on/after 1.1.2026. Source: [bshcpa reporting](https://www.bshcpa.co.il/ביטול-פטור-מדיווח-לתושבים-חוזרים/), [Arnon Tadmor-Levy](https://arnontl.com/he/news/ביטול-הפטור-מדיווח-על-הכנסות-פטורות-לע/), [Shibolet (EN)](https://www.shibolet.com/en/cancellation-of-reporting-exemption-for-new-immigrants-and-long-term-returning-residents-regarding-foreign-income-and-new-reporting-obligation-for-israeli-resident-trustees-regarding-trusts-not-requir/).
6. **What gets reported post-2026**: foreign income on Form 1301 + Nispach D-1, capital declarations on request, trust disclosures. Source: [Shibolet (EN)](https://www.shibolet.com/en/cancellation-of-reporting-exemption-for-new-immigrants-and-long-term-returning-residents-regarding-foreign-income-and-new-reporting-obligation-for-israeli-resident-trustees-regarding-trusts-not-requir/).
7. **Form 1348 disambiguation**: this form is for people LEAVING Israel declaring non-residency, NOT for returning residents. Returning residents file via Form 1301 + Schedule D-1. Source: [y-tax](https://y-tax.co.il/form-1348-residency-termination-form-residency-declaration/), [bshcpa 1348](https://www.bshcpa.co.il/טופס-1348/).
8. **Form 2409**: bank-level declaration of toshav chozer status. Banks typically request the form within 14 days of an incoming foreign deposit; this is bank convention, not statute. Source: [kolzchut](https://www.kolzchut.org.il/he/הטבות_במס_לתושבים_חוזרים_ששהו_בחו%22ל_מעל_10_שנים).
9. **Returning Resident Certificate** from Ministry of Aliyah and Integration: administrative document useful for absorption services. Does NOT grant tax benefits and does NOT bind the Tax Authority. Source: [bshcpa](https://www.bshcpa.co.il/תושב-חוזר-ותיק/).
10. **Section 14(b) year-of-acclimation election**: must be filed within 90 days of arrival via Form 1130. Miss the window and the deferral is unavailable. Source: [ITA Circular 1/2011](https://www.gov.il/BlobFolder/policy/income-tax-professional-inst-01-2011/he/Policy_IncomeTaxInst_hoz1-2011.pdf), [BSH CPA](https://www.bshcpa.co.il/שנת-הסתגלות-תושב-חוזר/).
11. **Hok Iddud Aliyah 2026 Israeli-source labor caps**: 600,000 NIS in 2026, 1,000,000 NIS in 2027-2028, 350,000 NIS in 2029, 150,000 NIS in 2030. Source: [Nevo statute](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law00/241397.htm), [BSH CPA](https://www.bshcpa.co.il/tax-exemption-returning-residents-olim-2026/).
12. **Israeli-source income table**: TASE, Israeli rentals, Israeli dividends, Israeli interest, Israeli business income all taxable throughout the 10-year window.
13. **Equity compensation sourcing**: RSUs and stock options are sourced by where work was performed during the VESTING period, not by grant date. Source: [S. Horowitz](https://s-horowitz.com/taxation-of-stock-options-for-employees-who-became-israeli-residents/).
14. **Capital losses during the exempt window**: not carry-forwardable against post-window gains under the section 92 matching principle. Source: [ITA Circular 10/2025](https://www.gov.il/BlobFolder/policy/professional-directives-271125-1/he/IncomeTax_professional-directives-271125-1.pdf), [Y-Tax commentary](https://y-tax.co.il/capital-loss-offsetting/).
15. **Center-of-life continuity**: 10 years of foreign residency must be continuous; multi-month sabbaticals back in Israel can break the chain. Source: [BSH CPA](https://www.bshcpa.co.il/תושב-חוזר-ותיק/).
16. **US citizens dual-tax trap**: Israeli section 14 exemption does NOT relieve US tax on worldwide income; US-Israel treaty saving clause (Article 6(3)) blocks treaty relief; no FTC because no Israeli tax was paid. Source: [Philip Stein on US-Israel treaty](https://www.pstein.com/our-firm/us-israel-tax-treaty/), [IRS treaty PDF](https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-trty/israel.pdf).
17. **Cash-flow projection methodology**: classify each income stream as exempt vs. taxable across the 10-year window (no actual shekel-amount tax computation, planning aid only).

## Should (touched lightly, deferred to CPA)

1. **CFC (Controlled Foreign Company) traps**: vatik exemption can be lost on dividends from foreign companies where the returnee controls >25%. Skill flags this but does not compute. Source: [bshcpa](https://www.bshcpa.co.il/תושב-חוזר-ותיק/).
2. **Trust traps**: foreign trusts where the returnee is settlor or beneficiary trigger reporting under the new 2026 regime even if tax-exempt.
3. **Year-of-acclimation election** under section 14(b) (shnat hashtagrut): defers residency status by one year.
4. **Israeli-source income earned during the 10-year window**: NOT exempt (regular Israeli rates apply).
5. **Pension treatment**: foreign pension income is exempt for vatik, but Israeli pension contribution credits interact with the exemption.
6. **Spouse not eligible for vatik**: a vatik returnee married to an Israeli citizen who never left does not extend vatik status to the spouse. Joint filing (תיק מאוחד) interactions are complex; consult a CPA.

## Out of scope

1. **Process/bureaucracy of returning** (Bituach Leumi, kupat cholim, driver's license, schools) → `israeli-returning-resident-navigator`.
2. **Vehicle import / customs / oleh rights on a car** → `israeli-returning-resident-customs-vehicle`.
3. **Olim chadashim** (new immigrants) tax benefits → `israeli-aliyah-navigator`. Note: vatik and oleh chadash get nearly identical tax treatment under section 14, but other benefits (Sal Klita, Hebrew ulpan etc.) differ.
4. **People LEAVING Israel** (relocation abroad, ניתוק תושבות) → `israeli-relocation-abroad`. Form 1348 belongs to that skill.
5. **Binding tax computation**: actual shekel-amount of tax owed, marginal-rate calculations, deduction optimization, foreign tax credit claims, treaty positions. This skill is a planning aid; users must engage a CPA before filing.
6. **Bituach Leumi treatment** of returning residents (siudi, kiztavat zikna eligibility resets etc.).
