# Rabbinate Marriage Registration Checklist

## Pre-Registration Checklist

### Documents to Gather

- [ ] **Teudat Zehut (ID card)** -- both partners, originals
- [ ] **3 passport photos** -- both partners, recent
- [ ] **Parents' Ketuba** -- both sets of parents, original or certified copy
  - If lost: request from the Rabbinate where parents married
  - If parents married abroad: may need additional Jewish status verification
- [ ] **Teudat Ravakut** (Certificate of Bachelorhood) -- only needed if registering outside your city of residence
  - Obtained from your local Rabbinate with: Teudat Zehut, passport photos, parents' ketuba, 2 witnesses
- [ ] **Venue kashrut certificate** -- must be from the local Religious Council (Rabbinate kashrut), NOT private kashrut (Badatz, Mehadrin, etc.)
- [ ] **Registration fee** -- 730-844 NIS depending on city (typically ~824 NIS in 2026); 40% discount available (430-506 NIS) for olim within 2 years, IDF/national service, students under 30, persons with disabilities

### Additional Documents (if applicable)

- [ ] **Birth certificate** -- required for olim (immigrants)
- [ ] **Conversion documents** -- original conversion papers + letter from converting rabbi
- [ ] **Divorce decree (get)** -- if previously married (Jewish divorce required)
- [ ] **Death certificate of previous spouse** -- if widowed

## Registration Process

### Step 1: Verify Eligibility
Before visiting the Rabbinate, confirm:
- [ ] Neither partner is a Kohen marrying a divorcee or convert
- [ ] Both partners can prove Jewish status
- [ ] Wedding date is NOT during Sefirat HaOmer or Three Weeks (unless specific exemptions apply)
- [ ] Wedding date is 21-90 days from today

### Step 2: Visit Religious Council
- [ ] Both partners appear together
- [ ] Bring all documents listed above
- [ ] Pay registration fee
- [ ] Complete interview with Rabbinate official

### Step 3: Post-Registration
- [ ] Receive confirmation of tik nisuin opening
- [ ] Mesader kiddushin assigned (or request specific rabbi)
- [ ] Schedule bride's mikveh visit (1-7 days before wedding)
- [ ] Arrange 2 kosher witnesses for ketuba signing (not blood relatives of either partner)

## Timeline

| When | Action |
|------|--------|
| 90 days before | Earliest to open tik nisuin |
| 60-90 days before | Ideal window to register |
| 21 days before | Latest to open tik nisuin |
| 7 days before | Bride's mikveh visit (flexible, 1-7 days) |
| Wedding day | Ketuba signing, chuppah ceremony |

## Common Issues and Solutions

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Parents' ketuba lost | Request copy from Rabbinate where they married |
| Parents married abroad | Bring additional proof of Jewish status (synagogue letter, birth certificates) |
| Kohen restrictions | Consult with Rabbinate before booking venue or setting date |
| Long wait at Rabbinate | Some cities have online appointment booking -- check your local Religious Council website |
| Venue kashrut is private only | Ask venue to obtain Rabbinate kashrut certificate, or choose a different venue |
