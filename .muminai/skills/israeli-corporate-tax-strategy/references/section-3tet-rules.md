# Section 3(tet), 3(tet1), and 3(yod) -- Deemed Interest Rules

## Legal Basis

Sections 3(tet) and 3(yod) of the Israeli Income Tax Ordinance establish tax liability on loans provided at below-market interest rates between related parties. Section 3(tet1) (Amendment 235, in force 2017) adds an automatic-reclassification rule for shareholder withdrawals that remain outstanding past a hard deadline.

## Section 3(tet) -- Non-CPI-Linked Loans

**2026 prescribed rate:** 6.53%

If a loan or credit is provided without interest or at interest below 6.53%, the difference is deemed taxable income to the borrower. For controlling shareholders, deemed interest is classified as employment income and reported on Form 126.

## Section 3(yod) -- CPI-Linked Loans

**2026 prescribed rate:** 4.9%

For loans between related parties that are CPI-linked, minimum real interest of 4.9% applies.

## Section 3(tet1) -- Automatic Reclassification of Shareholder Withdrawals

Enacted by Amendment 235 (29 Dec 2016, in force 1 Jan 2017). Detailed in Tax Authority Circular 7/2017.

**Rule:** A loan or withdrawal from a closely-held company to a controlling shareholder is automatically deemed withdrawn if not repaid by the **end of the tax year following the year of withdrawal**.

| Scenario | Deemed-withdrawal date |
|----------|----------------------|
| Loan drawn Jan 2026 | Dec 31, 2027 if not repaid |
| Loan drawn Nov 2026 | Dec 31, 2027 if not repaid |
| Loan drawn Jan 2027 | Dec 31, 2028 if not repaid |

**Cascade on deemed withdrawal (per Circular 7/2017):**

1. If the company has distributable retained earnings, the withdrawal is reclassified as a dividend (30% + any applicable surtax for controlling shareholders).
2. If no retained earnings, reclassified as salary (marginal rates + NI + employer NI).
3. Residual cases reclassified as a loan to a related party (subject to Section 3(tet) deemed-interest accrual).

**De minimis exemption:** Cumulative withdrawal balance below NIS 100,000 on every day of year N AND every day of year N-1 is outside §3(tet1).

**Section 3(tet1) also covers "use of asset" (שימוש בנכס):** Amendment 235 explicitly pulls in shareholder personal use of company-owned apartments, vehicles beyond limited business use, art, yachts, and similar assets, valued at deemed annual rental/usage value. This is the single most-missed §3(tet1) trap in real Tax Authority audits. A shareholder living in a company-owned apartment or driving a company-owned car for personal purposes accrues a deemed withdrawal even without a cash loan.

## Deemed Dividend Reclassification Risk

The Tax Authority may reclassify a shareholder loan as a deemed dividend when:

| Risk Factor | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| No written loan agreement | High risk |
| No repayment schedule | High risk |
| Loan used for personal purposes | Medium-high risk |
| Company has distributable retained earnings | Medium risk |
| Loan balance grows year over year | High risk |
| Loan still outstanding at end of year N+1 | Automatic under §3(tet1) |

**Consequences:** Entire loan taxed as dividend at 30% + potential surtax + interest and penalties.

## Documentation Requirements

1. Written loan agreement signed by both parties
2. Interest rate at least 6.53% (or documented reason for lower)
3. Repayment schedule with specific dates
4. Board resolution approving the loan
5. Proper bookkeeping entries
6. Evidence of actual repayments
7. Year-end review of all shareholder loan balances to track the §3(tet1) deadline
